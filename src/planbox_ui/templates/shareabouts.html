Shareabouts is a flexible tool for gathering public input on a map.

Collect local insights from neighborhood residents, or round up data at a state-wide scale. Bike networks, trash maps, street safety, participatory budgeting… Pick a Shareabouts that meets your needs.

Who's using Shareabouts?

Shareabouts has been used to gather public input in dozens of municipalities, for transportation planning, participatory budgeting, resiliency mapping and more. Check out some recent Shareabouts maps:

NYC DOT Vision Zero map

New York City is collecting feedback on street safety conditions like speeding and double parking, using Shareabouts and Google Street View.

CDOT Bike Parking Requests

Chicago residents can request a bike rack using Shareabouts. With a custom admin tool, city staff can review and reply to requests, and track installation tasks for contractors.

Miami Public Space Challenge

The Miami Foundation is discovering the best ideas for activating public spaces in Miami.

Boro Taxis

NYC’s Taxi and Limousine Commission recently gathered locations for new Boro Taxi stands.

Divvy Bikes

Divvy is asking Chicago for location suggestions, to help place 175 more bike share stations this year.

Virginia Highland Master Plan

Residents of Atlanta’s Virginia Highlands informed a community master plan, sharing what they see and know about the neighborhood on the map.

Make Brooklyn Safer

Neighbors are collecting information on dangerous intersections in Brooklyn, NY, to support advocacy with police and elected officials.

NY Rising

The New York Rising Community Reconstruction Program is using Shareabouts to identify projects and actions for rebuilding and resiliency in six NYC neighborhoods.

Participatory Budgeting in NYC

Residents of eight NYC council districts will directly decide how to spend $15 million of public money, with ideas coming in via Shareabouts and public events.

Configure What Information You Gather

  • Configure categories for submissions, differentiating them with map icons.

  • Collect additional information about participants—optionally gather name, zip code, and private contact info. Send automatic followup emails to keep people engaged.

  • Flexible surveys can ask open questions to collect detailed qualitative information. Or use checkboxes, drop-down lists, and yes/no questions to collet quantitative information. Or go visual and let users upload photographs.

  • Stay on top of your input with daily email reports. Explore, moderate, and download your data.

  • Link responses to other relevant map layers such as neighborhoods & districts to better understand the data.

Shareabouts not only helps build detailed data from local wisdom but it’s also effective for project promotion. A well-designed, rewarding public input tool helps your project reach more people. Shareabouts users can browse locations submitted by others, add comments and questions, and share them on Twitter & Facebook.
