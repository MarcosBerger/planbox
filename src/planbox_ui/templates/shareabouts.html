Features

Shareabouts lets you configure the kinds of information you want to gather:

  • Configure categories for different submissions, and differentiate them with icons on the map.

  • Collect additional information about participants—optionally, gather name, zip code, and private contact details.

  • With flexible surveys, ask multiple open questions to collect detailed qualitative information. Or require participants to use checkboxes, drop-down lists and yes/no questions to collet quantitative information.

  • Users can upload photographs when adding locations to the map.

  • Link responses to other relevant map layers, including neighborhoods and elected representatives’ districts. Use this to understand your data as it comes in.

Shareabouts not only helps build up detailed data from local wisdom but it is also effective for project promotion. A well-designed, rewarding public input tool helps your project reach more participants: participants using Shareabouts can browse locations submitted by other people, add comments and questions, and share them over Twitter and Facebook.

Open source

Shareabouts is an open source project. Developers can redeploy it and get involved on Github. Give feedback and shape what's coming next on our roadmap.

Who's using Shareabouts?

Shareabouts has been used to gather public input in dozens of municipalities, for transportation planning, participatory budgeting, resiliency mapping and more. Recent Shareabouts maps:

Miami Public Space Challenge

The Miami Foundation is discovering the best ideas for activating public spaces in Miami.

Boro Taxis

NYC’s Taxi and Limousine Commission recently gathered locations for new Boro Taxi stands.

Divvy Bikes

Divvy is asking Chicago for location suggestions, to help place 175 more bike share stations this year.

Virginia Highland Master Plan

Residents of Atlanta’s Virginia Highlands can help inform a community master plan, sharing what they see and know about the neighborhood on the map.

Make Brooklyn Safer

Neighbors are collecting information on dangerous intersections in Brooklyn, NY, to support advocacy with police and elected officials.

NY Rising

The New York Rising Community Reconstruction Program is using Shareabouts to identify projects and actions for rebuilding and resiliency in six NYC neighborhoods.

Participatory Budgeting in NYC

Residents of eight NYC council districts will directly decide how to spend $15 million of public money, with ideas coming in via Shareabouts and public events.
